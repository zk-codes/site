About The Questionnaire

The Proust Questionnaire, though named after French novelist Marcel Proust, he didn't create it. He answered a version, and his answers became famous after his death.

It has its origins in a Victorian parlor game called confession albums. These contained questions to be answered by guests to reveal their tastes and aspirations.

I discovered this delightful exercise through Xanthe Tynehorne's site. It's introspective, and the questions are specific enough to elicit interesting answers while remaining timeless.
