<p>Reflections from attending the Mildura Zine Fair in July 2024: my first zine fair and the first time I'd ever printed my zines.</p>
<ul>
    <li>Don't sell yourself short. People will like your zines!</li>
    <li>Bring zines to trade as an excuse for interesting conversations.</li>
    <li>Use the resources you have around you.</li>
    <li>Make zines on paper and clean them up and arrange them digitally.</li>
    <li>Print folded zines double-sided so there's an interesting surprise on the non-content side.</li>
    <li>You can sew bindings for zines with a sewing machine.</li>
    <li><a href="https://smallzinevolcano.bigcartel.com/" rel="noopener">Small Zine Volcano</a> accepts free zines for distribution in Melbourne and surrounds.</li>
    <li>Photocopying A4 (single-sided black and white) is cheapest.</li>
    <li>Zines can be anything and everything!</li>
</ul>
