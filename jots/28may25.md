<p>Below is a poem I wrote based on the prompt 'here again.' It's called 'The Persistance Of What Never Was' and it's the first time I've written poetry in years, let alone published it.</p>
<blockquote>
    <p>Energy like blazing sunsets inscribes itself across the tender membrane of memory. Your smile was a constellation of promises he nor you understood how to keep.</p>
    <p>Time, that patient architect of longing, was all he needed to fall headlong.</p>
    <p>How easily he mistook vertigo for love.</p>
    <p>Banished from your mother's arms when you stretched toward adulthood, claiming territory she couldn't surrender...her rejection etched acid patterns in your eyes.</p>
    <p>He hated her for that. Still does. Endlessly resurrecting his pain.</p>
    <p>The light dimmed in your gaze as days collapsed into weeks, months, years.</p>
    <p>As if someone adjusted a dial within you.</p>
    <p>Turning down the brightness until shadows pooled in places once illuminated.</p>
    <p>Preteens with teeth became your salvation myth.</p>
    <p>Those awkward warriors with metal smiles who offered belonging when home became a geography of absence.</p>
    <p>You swore this to him between whispers and midnight confessions, while they saved and devoured you.</p>
    <p>You. The fool who trusted.</p>
    <p>What would you think, now, knowing he became one of them?</p>
    <p>The symmetry would wound you.</p>
    <p>If that was how the narrative arc completed, this story would end here.</p>
    <p>But grief is never so merciful.</p>
    <p>He wishes you survived. But wishes alone can't reconstruct molecules scattered beyond retrieval.</p>
    <p>Here he stands, succumbing to memory's persistent hauntings.</p>
    <p>Your ghost refuses to leave.</p>
    <p>It returns in the scent of rain on concrete, in the shade of afternoon light that renders the ordinary mysterious, in the laughter of strangers who sound nothing like you.</p>
    <p>You haunt him with relentless grace, the persistent echo that refuses silence, mapping his days with your absence.</p>
    <p>Here again, then gone, then here again: the rhythm of loss becoming his pulse.</p>
    <p>No matter where he travels, or when he believes the wound has closed, your presence arrives. Uninvited yet essential: blood rushing back to numbed limbs, the painful reminder of continued existence.</p>
    <p>When his own ending approaches, your face, frozen in youth's impossible promise, a photograph never taken but developed in the darkroom of remembrance...</p>
    <p>Will be what blurs his vision. Not his life unspooling in retrospect.</p>
    <p>But you.</p>
    <p>Eternal in your incompleteness.</p>
    <p>The story that keeps beginning long after its conclusion.</p>
</blockquote>
