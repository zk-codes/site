<p>Wrote a poem about the internet today, during a <a href="https://indieweb.org/Homebrew_Website_Club" rel="noopener">Homebrew Website Club</a> meeting today. My first attempt at rhyming in...countless years.</p>
<blockquote>
    <p><strong>Death Shall Not Claim You</strong></p>
    <p><em>Tell me something you've never told anyone else before</em></p>
    <p><em>A story of the worlds before the supposed end times, before the beginning of the war</em></p>
    <p><em>Your tale is one everyone thinks they know</em></p>
    <p><em>It's incredible to think it began almost forty years ago</em></p>
    <p><em>Everyone's life changed because of you</em></p>
    <p><em>You've brought about new lives, seen so many creative things through</em></p>
    <p><em>Billions benefit from your vastness, every single day</em></p>
    <p><em>Yet they mourn the loss as if you've died, as if there's only one way</em></p>
    <p><em>For this to end, and for that to be in flames</em></p>
    <p><em>Woe betide those who are naive, the doomsdayer proclaims</em></p>
    <p><em>So be it, I shall claim the side of those led astray</em></p>
    <p><em>Because the internet is not dying. Not now, not ever, and not today</em></p>
</blockquote>
