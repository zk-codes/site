<p>Attended my first virtual author talk for while: Lisa Jewell's. Haven't read her books but it's always wonderful to hear from a writer.</p>
<ul>
    <li>Fascinating stories often come from unanswerable questions you ask the most.</li>
    <li>Novels often come from a singular, recurring image that won't let you go.</li>
    <li>Write with love. People will notice.</li>
    <li>Revisions follow the same paradox as time travel: woe if you meet your previous self.</li>
    <li>Sometimes the most fun characters to write are the most awful.</li>
    <li>The smallest things derail or make an entire story: use that to your advantage.</li>
    <li>There's nothing wrong with writing stories that continually focus on the same themes.</li>
    <li>A readership is a remarkable, delicate thing. Treat it with care.</li>
    <li>Social media isn't for sales, it's for strengthening relationships with your readers.</li>
    <li>Life lived through writing books.</li>
    <li>You pour yourself onto the page only to leave so much unsaid...the beauty of subtext.</li>
</ul>
